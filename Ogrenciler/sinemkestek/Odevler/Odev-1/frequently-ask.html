Real, raw honey is honey that is unprocessed straight from the bees. When flowers bloom bees collect the nectar and bring it back to the hive. They slowly and surely fill up their honey comb with that nectar and dry it in the process. The sticker it gets, the more honey-like it becomes. Honey is nectar! We don't pasteurize our honey, we just do a basic filter it for bits of wax and other nature bits.

Medical advice from the Government is that you shouldn't give honey to children under the age of 1 year or to anyone with an underdeveloped or compromised immune system due to the potential presence of the bacterium causing botulism

hough commercial honey prevails on the market, some retailers do offer healthier alternatives. To make sure you are buying raw honey, look at the label. It should say “100% raw” or “unpasteurized”. Words like “organic” and “pure” do not indicate that the honey was unheated. If the label passed the test, look at the product more closely.

It is recommended that people with diabetes may consume honey as a natural sweetener as a replacement to refined white or cane sugar if consumed in moderation and as part of a healthy diet(always consult a medical doctor or qualified nutritionist if you are concerned or have issues with insulin).

After a period of time, most real honey will crystallise.You can turn your honey into liquid again (if that’s how you prefer it) by standing it in a pot of heated water, out in the sun, or on a sunny windowsill and the crystals will melt. We recommend that you do not heat your honey in the microwave.
